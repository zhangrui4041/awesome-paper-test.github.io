Introduction

The digital transformation of industries has led to an unprecedented accumulation of data across various sectors, ranging from finance to healthcare. As organizations strive to harness the potential of big data, ensuring the integrity and authenticity of this data has emerged as a critical concern. Data breaches and unauthorized modifications pose substantial risks, threatening not only financial loss but also reputational damage and legal consequences. Traditional database management systems (DBMS), while sophisticated, often struggle to provide the guarantee of immutability that is increasingly demanded in contemporary data governance frameworks. Recent developments in blockchain technology offer promising solutions by introducing a decentralized model of data validation, characterized by a secure and transparent ledger system. Unlike traditional systems that rely on centralized control, blockchain employs cryptographic techniques and consensus algorithms to ensure that once data is recorded, it cannot be altered retroactively. This paper seeks to investigate the integration of blockchain technology within database systems to enhance data integrity without sacrificing the operational efficiency and scalability expected from enterprise-grade solutions. We begin by reviewing existing literature on the limitations faced by current DBMS in maintaining data integrity in high-risk environments. Furthermore, we draw comparisons with blockchain’s fundamental principles of decentralization and immutability, discussing how they can be leveraged to address these limitations. Our research proposes a hybrid database architecture that synergizes the robustness of blockchain with the efficiency of traditional databases. Through this innovative framework, we aim to provide a path forward for businesses looking to secure sensitive data against modern cyber threats. The introduction of blockchain-based immutability within database systems represents not just a technological advancement, but a paradigm shift in how data integrity can be envisioned and executed. By examining case studies and conducting performance evaluations, our study offers a comprehensive analysis of the potential benefits and challenges of adopting such an approach. This work aspires to inform policy makers, IT professionals, and database engineers about the future trajectory of database security and inspire further research in this exciting and crucial domain.
