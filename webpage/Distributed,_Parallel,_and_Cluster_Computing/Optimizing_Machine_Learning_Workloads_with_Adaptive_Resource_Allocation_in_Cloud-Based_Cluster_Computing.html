This paper presents a novel approach to optimizing machine learning workloads in cloud-based cluster computing environments through adaptive resource allocation. With the increasing prevalence of data-intensive applications and the demand for real-time processing, efficient resource management becomes crucial. Our approach leverages machine learning techniques to predict workload patterns and adapts resource allocation dynamically to enhance performance and minimize costs. We implemented this method in a cloud environment and conducted extensive experiments using a variety of workload scenarios. Introduction

The surge in the utilization of machine learning (ML) applications necessitates robust and efficient computing infrastructures to process vast amounts of data timely and effectively. Cloud-based cluster computing has emerged as a pivotal solution, providing scalability, flexibility, and high availability required for such complex tasks. However, managing resources in these distributed environments to optimize machine learning workloads remains a significant challenge. Traditionally, resource allocation in cloud clusters is conducted statically, often leading to either over-provisioning, where resources are underutilized, or under-provisioning, which results in processing delays and degradation in application performance. Recent advancements propose leveraging adaptive resource management strategies powered by intelligent decision-making capabilities to overcome these drawbacks. These strategies exploit real-time workload predictions and dynamic adjustments to resource allocation, thereby reducing costs and enhancing system responsiveness. Despite these potential advantages, research and implementation in real-world settings are limited. This paper introduces an innovative approach that integrates machine learning-driven predictive analytics for dynamic resource adaptation in cloud-based clusters. By preemptively understanding workload fluctuations, our solution intelligently allocates resources to prevent bottlenecks and optimize utilization. We document the design, implementation, and evaluation of our method, which showcases tangible improvements in efficiency and cost-effectiveness compared to conventional allocation strategies. Through extensive evaluation using diverse workload patterns, we illustrate the viability and advantages of our approach. Furthermore, this research aligns with the broader goals of distributed computing, which seeks to enhance operational efficiency while catering to the growing demands of large-scale data processing. Our findings not only contribute to academic discourse but also provide practical insights for industry applications, especially for organizations aiming to streamline their cloud-based distributed systems amidst rapidly evolving computational landscapes.
