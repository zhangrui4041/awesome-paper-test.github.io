This paper explores the innovative integration of deep reinforcement learning (DRL) into symbolic computation for enhanced efficiency and capability in solving complex algebraic problems. We present a novel framework that leverages DRL to optimize symbolic reasoning tasks, offering a dynamic approach that adapts to a vast array of symbolic challenges. Introduction

Symbolic computation, a pivotal domain within computer science, focuses on the manipulation and solving of mathematical expressions in symbolic form. This area has been instrumental in various applications, from solving algebraic equations to developing complex computer-aided design systems. Traditionally, symbolic computation relied on established algorithms and rule-based systems, which, despite their effectiveness, often struggle with scalability and the flexibility needed to tackle increasingly complex problems efficiently. In recent years, the advent of artificial intelligence, particularly deep learning, has revolutionized many fields of computation. While much of AI’s success has been attributed to data-driven, statistical methods, incorporating these advancements into symbolic computation presents an exciting frontier yet to be fully realized. Among these innovative AI methodologies, deep reinforcement learning (DRL) offers a promising avenue for dynamically improving symbolic problem-solving capabilities through learning-based approaches. DRL has proven notably effective in dynamic environments where strategy and adaptation to new information are crucial, as evidenced by its transformative impact in fields such as robotics, gaming, and autonomous systems. These same characteristics make DRL an intriguing candidate for symbolic computation, an area traditionally dominated by static rule-based processes. By allowing for adaptive learning, DRL may enable symbolic systems to transcend current limitations, delivering faster and more accurate solutions. Our research introduces a framework that amalgamates DRL with symbolic computation. This system dynamically optimizes the decision-making processes inherent in symbolic reasoning tasks, allowing for enhanced performance in algebraic problem solving. In our experiments, we focus on algebraic benchmarks that depict real-world challenges faced in computational mathematics. By doing so, we illustrate how our framework can serve as a new benchmark for the efficiency and robustness of symbolic computation tasks. We begin with a review of the current state of symbolic computation technologies and identify limitations that our proposed solution aims to address. We then delve into the technical architecture of our DRL-enhanced symbolic computation model, followed by a detailed analysis of experimental results and a discussion on potential applications and future directions. This research aims to pave the way for more sophisticated symbolic computation systems, bridging the current methods with next-generation AI advancements.
