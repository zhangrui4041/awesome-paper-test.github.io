With the proliferation of cloud computing technologies, cloud-based operating systems are becoming an integral part of modern IT infrastructure. Despite their benefits in terms of scalability and cost-effectiveness, they pose significant security and privacy challenges. This paper investigates current trends in security threats targeting cloud-based operating systems and evaluates various techniques designed to enhance privacy and ensure data security. By conducting a comprehensive literature review and analyzing recent advancements in this field, our study identifies and assesses the effectiveness of these techniques in mitigating potential threats. The paper also highlights the practicality of these measures in real-world applications and suggests future research directions for further improvements. Introduction

In recent years, cloud computing has transformed the landscape of information technology, offering unparalleled opportunities for scalability, flexibility, and cost-efficiency. Cloud-based operating systems (CBOS) are at the forefront of this revolution, providing the backbone for diverse applications across industries. However, despite their advantages, CBOS continue to present significant security and privacy risks, which demand rigorous scrutiny and the development of robust protective measures. The intricacy of cloud environments, characterized by multi-tenancy, data distribution, and dynamic resource allocation, introduces vulnerabilities that can be exploited by malicious actors leading to unauthorized data access, data breaches, and service disruptions. This paper delves into the evolving threat landscape confronting cloud-based operating systems and emphasizes the essential need for enhanced privacy and security strategies. While cloud service providers strive to secure their infrastructures, the responsibility also falls on the system architects and developers to implement robust security measures within CBOS. This study reviews cutting-edge literature to present a synthesized understanding of the common threat vectors affecting these systems, ranging from Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attacks to vulnerabilities in virtualization. Innovative privacy enhancement techniques such as homomorphic encryption, secure multi-party computation, and advanced intrusion detection systems are explored to determine their efficacy in addressing these security challenges. Furthermore, the paper discusses current best practices and strategies adopted by leading cloud service companies, such as Amazon Web Services and Microsoft Azure, to shed light on effective methodologies for bolstering security. Motivated by the critical role of cloud computing in contemporary software infrastructures and the rising incidence of cyber threats, this paper aims to serve as a comprehensive resource. It seeks to equip information security professionals, system developers, and academic researchers with valuable insights and practical guidance to fortify cloud-based operating systems against persistent and emerging threats, ultimately fostering a more secure digital ecosystem.
