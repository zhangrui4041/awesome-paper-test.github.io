As healthcare systems transition towards digital environments, there is an increasing need to personalize patient care while ensuring data privacy and security. Federated learning (FL) offers a transformative approach to achieve these goals, as it allows for collaborative learning across multiple healthcare institutions without the need to share sensitive patient data. This paper explores the application of federated learning to create personalized healthcare models that can improve diagnostic accuracy and treatment efficacy. We review existing literature, highlight the technical and ethical challenges inherent to deploying FL in healthcare, and propose novel solutions to overcome these barriers. Our analysis suggests that while federated learning holds significant promise for advancing personalized healthcare, success will require collaborative efforts across technologies, policies, and regulations.

Introduction

The integration of Artificial Intelligence (AI) within healthcare has ushered in a new era of personalized medicine, promising to enhance patient outcomes through tailored diagnostics and treatments. Central to this innovation is the vast repository of patient data, which, when effectively harnessed, can reveal patterns and insights instrumental for medical advancements. However, the sensitive nature of health data mandates strict compliance with privacy regulations and ethical considerations, creating a paradox for leveraging AI in a privacy-conscious healthcare environment. Federated learning (FL) emerges as a pioneering solution designed to navigate this dilemma by enabling institutions to collaboratively train AI models without transferring patient data offsite. In federated learning, data remains decentralized, allowing models to learn from data across multiple healthcare providers while keeping patient information secure on local servers. This characteristic is vital for healthcare, where data breach risks and privacy concerns are particularly heightened. However, deploying federated learning in healthcare applications presents distinct challenges, including technical complexities, interoperability issues, and regulatory hurdles. This paper offers a comprehensive exploration of federated learning's role in fostering personalized healthcare. By reviewing recent advancements and providing a thorough analysis of ongoing challenges, we aim to paint a clear picture of the future possibilities FL can unlock in the healthcare domain. We also discuss various solutions and strategies to address key challenges, ensuring that the deployment of federated learning systems are both effective and compliant. Herein, we propose that the successful implementation of FL in healthcare will necessitate not only technical innovation but also cooperative strategies among stakeholders including healthcare providers, technologists, and policymakers. The main thrust of this work is to establish a roadmap guiding the use of federated learning to drive the next phase of personalized healthcare, emphasizing collaboration, security, and efficacy as crucial pillars for future development.
