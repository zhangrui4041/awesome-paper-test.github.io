Introduction

In the age of information, the explosion of data generation has posed significant challenges and opportunities for the field of computer science. Big data analytics, once at the forefront of technological advancements, is now confronted with issues related to speed, accuracy, and scalability. With traditional computational resources reaching their limits, there is a growing need for novel methodologies capable of tackling the vast datasets characterizing modern data environments. Quantum Machine Learning (QML), an interdisciplinary field that merges the computational superiority of quantum computing with the versatility of machine learning algorithms, emerges as a promising solution to these challenges. Quantum computers leverage quantum bits or qubits, enabling them to perform complex calculations more efficiently than classical computers. This unique capability is particularly advantageous for scenarios requiring the manipulation of large datasets, as in big data analytics. The fusion of quantum computing and machine learning points towards the potential of executing tasks that would be infeasible for classical systems, such as real-time data analysis and adaptive learning processes in dynamic environments. The purpose of this research paper is to delve into the realm of QML, specifically focusing on its applications in enhancing big data analytics. We will investigate how current quantum algorithms can synergize with machine learning techniques to push the boundaries of what is computationally feasible. Our journey begins with a comprehensive literature review, examining existing works in the domain of QML applied to big data. By identifying gaps and limitations in current methodologies, the paper proposes a hybrid computational model that leverages both quantum advantages and classical robustness, aiming to achieve higher efficiency and accuracy in data analysis. This paper not only identifies the technological milestones but also discusses the broader implications for industries reliant on data insights, such as finance, healthcare, and logistics. Through a structured exploration, the study aspires to contribute valuable perspectives to the growing discourse on integrating quantum computing capabilities within machine learning frameworks, paving the way for groundbreaking efficiencies in big data analytics.
