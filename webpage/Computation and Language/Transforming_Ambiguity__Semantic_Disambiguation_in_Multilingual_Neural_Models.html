Introduction

The advent of machine learning and neural networks has significantly advanced the field of natural language processing (NLP), reshaping how computers interpret human languages. Yet, one of the persistent challenges in NLP is semantic ambiguity, where words or phrases can have multiple meanings depending on context. This issue becomes especially pronounced in multilingual environments, where direct translations between languages often fail to capture the precise meaning intended by a speaker. The need to address semantic ambiguity in NLP is critical, not just for improving machine translation but also for enhancing various applications such as sentiment analysis, chatbots, and information retrieval systems. Recent developments in transformer architectures, particularly models like BERT and GPT, have demonstrated superior capabilities in understanding contextualized word representations. These models, however, still grapple with nuanced semantic disambiguations, especially when applied across multiple languages. The current research aims to bridge this gap by introducing an innovative framework designed to improve semantic disambiguation in multilingual neural models. This framework is built on the premise that integrating dedicated semantic layers within existing transformer models can markedly enhance their ability to discern the correct meaning of ambiguous words based on contextual cues. To develop this framework, we focus on integrating state-of-the-art semantic disambiguation layers that actively interact with multilingual neural networks. By doing so, our model can effectively understand and process language semantics regardless of linguistic complexities. The proposed method is evaluated using a variety of multilingual datasets across ten languages, encompassing differing language structures and cultural contexts. Our experiments show significant improvements in model performance, as evidenced by enhanced accuracy in machine translation and reduced ambiguity in sentiment analysis tasks. This paper is structured as follows: we first review relevant literature that has tackled similar challenges in multilingual NLP. We then detail the architecture of our proposed framework, followed by an analysis of the results obtained from our empirical testing. Finally, we discuss the implications of our findings for future research in NLP and potential applications in global communication technologies. The ultimate goal of this research is to facilitate more effective and accurate language processing, thereby contributing to the broader vision of seamless international communication in the digital age.
