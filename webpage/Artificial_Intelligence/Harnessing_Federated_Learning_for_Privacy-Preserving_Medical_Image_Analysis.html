Harnessing Federated Learning for Privacy-Preserving Medical Image Analysis

Dr. Emily Tran1, Dr. Sanjay Patel2, Dr. Linh Nguyen3, Dr. Marcus Klein4,

Abstract

The advent of Artificial Intelligence in the healthcare domain has opened new vistas for medical diagnostics, particularly through image analysis. However, the deployment of AI in this sensitive field is often hindered by privacy concerns surrounding patient data. Federated Learning (FL) presents an innovative approach to mitigate these concerns by enabling model training directly on decentralized data sources without transferring raw data. This paper explores the application of Federated Learning in medical image analysis, highlighting its potential to improve diagnostic capabilities while ensuring data privacy compliance. We present a comprehensive review of current federated learning algorithms, their applicability in handling diverse imaging data, and adapt them to address real-world healthcare challenges. The effectiveness of the proposed framework is validated through extensive experiments on multimodal medical datasets, showcasing the balance it strikes between preserving patient confidentiality and enhancing diagnostic performance. Our findings underscore the transformative impact of Federated Learning in advancing AI's role in medical imaging while upholding privacy standards.

Introduction

The field of medical imaging has experienced a paradigm shift with the introduction of Artificial Intelligence (AI) technologies. From radiology to dermatology, AI-driven image analysis has significantly improved diagnostic accuracy and speed. Despite these advancements, the widespread integration of AI in healthcare is impeded by challenges related to patient privacy and data security. Healthcare data is richly informative yet inherently sensitive, demanding rigorous methods to protect patient privacy. Traditional centralized AI training approaches, which require aggregation of raw data into centralized servers, pose substantial risks, including data breaches and non-compliance with privacy regulations like HIPAA and GDPR. Federated Learning (FL) emerges as a promising solution, decentralizing the training process by keeping the data on local devices and only sending model updates to a central server. This paradigm not only addresses privacy concerns but also allows collaborations across institutions which can lead to enriched model training with diverse datasets. Previous studies mainly focused on the technical feasibility of FL across different sectors, but there remains a gap in effectively integrating FL for medical imaging tasks while considering stringent privacy demands. This paper aims to fill that gap by proposing a novel framework leveraging Federated Learning in medical image analysis. We delve into the review of existing federated learning techniques, examining their strengths and limitations in the context of healthcare. Special attention is paid to the unique challenges posed by medical data, such as heterogeneity and label scarcity. Furthermore, we address the adaptability of FL algorithms to effectively process and analyze diverse imaging modalities, from MRI scans to histopathological images. Through rigorous experiments using multimodal datasets, we demonstrate the potential of FL to enhance predictive performance while adhering to privacy constraints. This study not only contributes to advancing federated learning methodologies but also aligns with ethical data use, aiming to harmonize technological and humanitarian goals in contemporary healthcare.
