This paper presents a novel approach to swarm robotics by integrating cognitive architectures to enhance adaptability in dynamic environments. Current swarm systems are often limited by their reliance on static algorithms, which restrict their ability to respond to unforeseen changes or complex, evolving tasks. Drawing inspiration from cognitive neuroscience, our research introduces a biologically inspired adaptive framework that allows a swarm of robots to learn and reorganize their behaviors autonomously and collaboratively. Introduction

The field of swarm robotics has witnessed significant advancements over the past decade, primarily driven by the burgeoning interest in utilizing large numbers of simple robots to perform tasks that require adaptability and scalability. Traditional swarm systems rely on simple rule-based algorithms, which while effective in stable and predictable environments, often falter when faced with dynamic and unpredictable scenarios. The limitations of static programming in such contexts highlight the urgent need for adaptable frameworks that can empower robotic systems to modify their behaviors autonomously based on real-time feedback. This paper proposes an innovative approach by integrating cognitive architectures in swarm robotics, which draws parallels from cognitive neuroscience. Cognitive architectures provide a blueprint for artificial intelligence systems that aspire to replicate the flexibility and learning capabilities found in biological organisms. By adopting neural plasticity principles, our framework encourages individual robots to adaptively learn from their environment and enhance collective decision-making processes. Recent studies in artificial intelligence and neuroscience indicate that incorporating learning models from the human brain, such as synaptic adaptability, can significantly boost the cognitive capabilities of artificial systems. This integration not only allows individual robots within a swarm to process environmental stimuli more effectively but also enables the synergy of knowledge, facilitating an emergent intelligence that is greater than the sum of its parts. Our research engages deeply with contemporary literature, exploring existing techniques and highlighting foundational studies that advocate for the convergence of cognitive sciences with robotic design. By showcasing both the theoretical foundations and practical implementations, this paper sheds new light on the future of adaptive swarm robotics. The approach is validated through rigorous simulations and experimental trials conducted in diverse environments exhibiting varying degrees of complexity and uncertainty. The results demonstrate a marked improvement in the ability of robotic swarms to manage tasks efficiently while adapting to unforeseen changes. These findings not only advance the theoretical understanding of swarm robotics but also propose practical implications for applications ranging from disaster response and environmental monitoring to logistics and agriculture. Ultimately, by converging cognitive science with robotics, we endeavor to create robust systems capable of autonomous decision-making and adaptability, paving the way for more intelligent and resilient robotic swarms.
