Introduction

The ever-increasing volume of data generated by modern applications challenges traditional data storage and retrieval techniques. As industries rely heavily on data-driven decision making, there is a growing demand for sophisticated methods that can manage and process large datasets efficiently. Traditional static indexing methods, while effective in smaller datasets, falter under the pressure of dynamically evolving systems characterized by big data. This necessitates the development of advanced dynamic indexing techniques that can provide fast and reliable data access while maintaining scalability and efficiency. Dynamic indexing, unlike static methods, allows for real-time updates without the need for complete re-structuring, making it ideal for environments where data are continuously generated and modified. Recent advancements in this domain have shown promise in overcoming the bottlenecks faced by conventional systems. However, these methods are often limited by their dependency on the specific characteristics of the datasets they are applied to or the computational resources available. This paper aims to explore a wide spectrum of dynamic indexing techniques, evaluating their performance and limitations. By comparing contemporary methods across various big data scenarios, we aim to uncover insights into their adaptability, efficiency, and performance overheads. Additionally, this paper introduces a novel hybrid indexing approach, blending existing paradigms to harness their strengths while mitigating their weaknesses. Our experimental evaluations on diverse real-world datasets reveal that this hybrid model significantly enhances retrieval times and system scalability, demonstrating its potential as a viable solution for modern data-intensive applications. In essence, this work not only underscores the critical need for evolving data structures in the face of big data challenges but also actively contributes to the growing body of research focused on creating more robust, efficient, and scalable solutions for data retrieval. Advancements in this area promise to expand the potential of big data analytics and information systems, ultimately leading to more informed and swift decision-making processes across various domains.
