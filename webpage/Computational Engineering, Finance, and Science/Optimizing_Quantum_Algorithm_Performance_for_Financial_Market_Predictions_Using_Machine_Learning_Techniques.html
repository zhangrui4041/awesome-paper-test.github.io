Introduction

The ever-increasing complexity of financial markets and the vast amount of data generated daily pose significant challenges to traditional computational models used for market predictions. As global financial markets continue to evolve, there is a pressing need for more advanced computational approaches that can analyze large datasets efficiently and make accurate predictions swiftly. The advent of quantum computing presents a promising avenue for addressing these challenges due to its potential to solve complex computational problems significantly faster than classical computers. In recent years, the intersection of quantum computing and machine learning has garnered considerable interest. Quantum computing’s ability to process information in fundamentally different ways opens new possibilities for enhancing machine learning algorithms. These enhancements are particularly pertinent to financial market predictions, where speed and accuracy are paramount. However, despite the theoretical advantages promised by quantum computing, practical implementations and optimizations tailored for financial predictions remain under-explored. This paper explores an innovative approach that combines quantum computing with advanced machine learning techniques for predicting financial market trends. Our work focuses on optimizing quantum algorithms for improved performance in identifying and forecasting market movements. We propose a hybrid model that uses quantum-enhanced feature selection to refine input data, funneling it through classical machine learning models for prediction. This model leverages quantum computing’s massive parallelism and computational power to accelerate data processing and improve the accuracy of financial forecasts. The paper is structured as follows: first, we review the current state of quantum computing applications in finance, highlighting the strengths and weaknesses uncovered in recent studies. Next, we describe our hybrid model, detailing the quantum and classical machine learning components and how they are integrated. We then present our empirical results based on extensive back-testing against market data, demonstrating the model’s enhanced prediction capabilities. Finally, we discuss the implications of our findings for the broader fields of computational finance and quantum computing, and propose directions for future research. By addressing existing gaps in this field, this research paves the way for more sophisticated applications of emerging technologies in financial analytics.
