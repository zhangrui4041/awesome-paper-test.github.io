With the rapid development of quantum computing, the study of traditional combinatorial optimization problems within the quantum computing framework has become an emerging research frontier. This paper explores the potential synergy between quantum algorithms and classical graph theory, aiming to optimize the solution process for combinatorial problems in graph theory using quantum acceleration. First, we review several fundamental problems in classical graph theory, such as maximum matching, shortest paths, and graph coloring, analyzing their computational complexity and the limitations of traditional algorithms. We then propose a novel quantum algorithmic framework that combines quantum superposition and interference principles to approximate solutions to these problems. By leveraging the speedup inherent in quantum algorithms, we aim to achieve better performance in solving specific types of graph structures than with classical algorithms. Finally, experimental results validate the performance improvements of quantum algorithms in solving particular graph theory problems, such as graph coloring and maximum matching, comparing them with existing classical optimization methods. The findings suggest that quantum computing could serve as an effective complement to classical graph theory algorithms, offering promising new avenues for the advancement of the field.
